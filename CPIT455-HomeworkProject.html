Delivery Application Assessment

The system I am assessing is a delivery application. It is used by customers to order food, by restaurants to receive and prepare orders, and by drivers to deliver them. The system follows an availability-focused design because it must provide its services at all times. Since it operates online, users expect it to function 24/7 without interruptions.

The delivery application has a dynamic usage pattern. Traffic typically spikes between 12:00 PM and 3:00 PM during lunchtime, and again between 7:00 PM and 10:00 PM during dinner time. During these peak hours, the system must handle a large number of simultaneous users, orders, and payment transactions. Therefore, scalability and performance are very important to maintain smooth operation.

The most critical dependability attribute for this system is availability. If the system goes down, customers cannot place orders, restaurants cannot receive requests, and drivers cannot access delivery details. This would result in financial losses, damaged reputation, and customer dissatisfaction. Reliability is also important, as orders must not be lost or duplicated, and payments must be processed correctly.

In addition, the system must ensure security by protecting user data such as addresses, phone numbers, and payment information. Any data breach could significantly harm user trust. Maintainability is also crucial to allow updates and feature additions without disrupting service. Overall, the delivery application must be highly dependable to maintain continuous service, especially during peak demand periods.

Proposed Strategy – Microservices Architecture

To further improve dependability, the system can adopt a microservices architecture, where each major component—customer ordering, restaurant management, and driver delivery—is implemented as an independent service. This separation allows each service to scale, update, and fail independently, improving availability, maintainability, and performance while ensuring continuous operation even under peak load.

⚖️ Safety vs Reliability — My understanding

"A system can perfectly follow its specification and still cause harm. Safety is freedom from harm, Reliability is conformance to spec. A reliable weapon is still dangerous — that insight changed everything."
